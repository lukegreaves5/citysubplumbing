The most common reason for a leaking faucet is from corroding o-rings or valve seats. If one of these components is corroded, the faucet can't hold the water when it is closed, therefore, resulting in a leaking and dripping faucet.

To fix a leaking faucet, shut off the water and remove the faucet. Check for any visible corrosion around the sorrounding components of the faucet, you can find replacements at your local hardware store. If there is no sign of corrosion, check for any loose screws - tightening anything loose may be enough to temporarily stop the leak. If these steps fails, give us a call!
If there is water dripping from the bottom of the water heater, it is usually a sign of a crack in the tank which requires a water heater tank replacement.
A lack of hot water does not usually require a full water heater replacement, however, you may needs to replace some smaller components.

If you have an electric water heater, the heating elements or thermostat may have expired and need replacing. In the case of both electric and gas water hearts, a lack of hot water could be cue to the dip tube that has detached and deposited itself into the tank, allowing cold water to be mixed with hot water which does not allow for the water to heat up to the desired temperature.
